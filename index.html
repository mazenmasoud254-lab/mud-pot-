Complete Electronic Components

All parts required to build your own mud pot air cooler

Terracotta Clay Pot

The heart of the cooling system. Made from natural porous clay, it absorbs and retains water for continuous evaporative cooling.

Material Natural Terracotta
Size Medium (1.5L)
Function Cooling Element

PC Cooling Fan (120mm)

Standard computer case fan with 4-pin connector. Provides airflow for the evaporative cooling process.

Voltage 5V DC
Current 0.2A
Speed 1500 RPM

USB Cable & Connector

Standard USB-A to 4-pin fan connector cable. Powers the fan from any USB power source (laptop, power bank, adapter).

Type USB-A Male
Length 1 Meter
Connector 4-pin PWM

Digital Thermometer

Mini digital thermometer to measure temperature drop. Shows cooling effectiveness from 34°C to 26°C as in video.

Range -50°C to 70°C
Accuracy ±1°C
Display LCD Digital

USB Power Source

Can use laptop USB port, power bank, or USB wall adapter. Provides 5V DC power to run the cooling fan.

Voltage 5V DC
Current 1-2A
Options Laptop/Power Bank

Connecting Wires & Tools

Basic electrical wires, soldering iron (optional), and hot glue gun for assembly and securing components.

Wire Gauge 22 AWG
Tools Hot Glue Gun
Optional Soldering Iron

Step-by-Step Assembly Guide

Follow these simple steps to build your mud pot cooler

1. Prepare the Pot

Soak the terracotta pot in water for 2 hours to fully saturate the clay pores.

2. Connect Fan to USB

Connect the 4-pin fan connector to the USB cable. Match red wire to +5V, black to ground.

3. Mount the Fan

Position the fan above the pot to pull air through the wet clay surface.

4. Power Connection

Connect USB to power source (laptop, power bank, or USB adapter).

5. Temperature Testing

Use digital thermometer to measure temperature drop (34°C → 26°C).

6. Final Assembly

Secure all components with hot glue and ensure proper airflow direction.
